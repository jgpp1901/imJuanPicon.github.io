Fixed Income & Risk Management

I possess a deep technical understanding of fixed-income instruments and the mechanics of the yield curve. My experience includes constructing spot and forward rate curves to calculate nominal and Z-spreads, as well as quantifying interest rate sensitivity through Macaulay duration and convexity metrics. Furthermore, I have experience modeling complex debt structures, including mortgage amortization and the tranches of Collateralized Mortgage Obligations (CMOs). By applying binomial interest rate trees, I can accurately value both option-free bonds and those with embedded options.

Derivative Securities

I have developed a strong technical foundation in the valuation and application of derivative instruments to identify market inefficiencies. By utilizing theoretical forward pricing models, I have analyzed markets to pinpoint and exploit arbitrage opportunities. My expertise includes the practical application of the Black–Scholes model and multi-step binomial tree frameworks to price options, allowing for a comprehensive assessment of payoff structures, "Greeks," and the impact of volatility on derivative pricing.

Quantitative Risk Management

My approach to risk management is grounded in advanced statistical modeling and volatility forecasting. I have experience constructing diversified portfolios and quantifying risk through the calculation of individual asset volatilities and correlations. To capture market dynamics accurately, I apply Exponentially Weighted Moving Averages (EWMA) and GARCH models, alongside Extreme Value Theory (EVT) to account for fat-tail risks. These methodologies culminate in the calculation of Value at Risk (VaR), providing a rigorous framework for measuring and mitigating potential portfolio losses under various market conditions.
